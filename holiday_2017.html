Peace Corps Champion

Each year, RPCV/W awards its Peace Corps Champion Award to an individual whose work has made a great impact on the Peace Corps and its community.

Last Year's Recipient

Chic Dambach

Chic Dambach served in Colombia from 1967 – 1969 where he worked with local fisherman to protect coral reefs while preserving their livelihoods. He went on to advise the executive staffs and governing boards of prominent national and local nonprofits and publish books on the subject of effective governance. He led the National Peace Corps Association from 1992 – 1998, and played a key role in brokering a peace agreement to the Eritrean–Ethiopian War in 2000. He organized numerous initiatives to develop and advance the cause of sustainable peace and security worldwide as the CEO of Alliance for Peacebuilding, and later served as Chief of Staff for Congressman John Garamendi, RPCV Ethiopia. Though technically retired, he lectures at Johns Hopkins and serves on several nonprofit boards and advisory boards. His early story is chronicled in his bestselling memoir: Exhaust the Limits, the Life and Times of a Global Peacebuilder. He was recently nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize.

Returned Peace Corps Volunteers of Washington, D.C. (RPCV/W) is a member-driven organization committed to the Peace Corps’ Third Goal: Promoting a better understanding of the peoples of the world within our local community. It was founded in 1979 and incorporated in 1990.

Representing more than 3,000 returned Peace Corps Volunteers, staff, families and friends, RPCV/W is one of the largest and most active organizations of its kind. All are welcome to join.
